## Transcription Notes

1. This transcription was made from a scanned PDF of the original 1949 tract map. The scan quality varies across sheets; many fine dimensions, bearings, and curve data are partially illegible due to age, fold marks, and scan resolution.

2. All items marked [?] indicate uncertainty in the reading. Some bearings, distances, and names may be slightly different from what is shown.

3. The map uses standard surveying notation of the era: bearings in degrees/minutes/seconds, distances in feet, curves described by radius/arc/delta.

4. Sheet 2 contains the densest concentration of lot data (Lots 22-73). Sheet 3 covers the coastal bluff lots (Lots 2-6) and the upper northern lots (74-81). Sheet 4 covers the Cove Road lots (7-21) and upper Packet Road lots (42-48).

5. The "NOT A PART OF THIS SUBDIVISION" area appears on both Sheets 2 and 3, in the area between Clipper Road and Palos Verdes Drive South on the western side. This area later becomes relevant to the Lot H / 0 Clipper controversy documented elsewhere in the archive.

6. The basis of bearings (N 81 50'15" W along PV Drive South centerline per C.S.B. 1084-2) is critical for any survey retracement. NOTE: CSB 1084-2 is the county surveyor's Map of Lots H and J from the Bixby partition (October 1935, Alfred Jones, County Surveyor). This means the tract map shares the same coordinate system as the Lot H/J boundary map.

7. The bond amount for the subdivision was $1,000.00 as noted in the Board of Supervisors acceptance.

8. The civil engineer certified monuments would be in place no later than July 1, 1949, based on a survey conducted in November 1947.
