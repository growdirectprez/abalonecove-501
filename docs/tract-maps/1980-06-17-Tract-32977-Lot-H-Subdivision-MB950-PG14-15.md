## Summary

Tract Map No. 32977 is the 1980 subdivision of a portion of Lot H in the Rancho Palos Verdes area into five lots, filed June 17, 1980 with the LA County Registrar-Recorder. The subdivision was a residential planned development with four residential lots (1-4) and one common-area lot (Lot 5) held by a mandatory homeowners association. Lot 5 (the common area, ~1 acre) was partially within a designated flood hazard zone. This subdivision was later reversed by Tract 43725 in 1986, which merged all five lots back into a single parcel.

## Key Provisions

- **Origin:** Subdivision arose from a decree of partition in action "Bixby et al. vs. Bent et al." filed in District Court (Case #C 2975), entered in Book 9, Page 87 of Judgments in the Superior Court of LA County
- **Owner:** Kenneth K. Wong, with Alice V.L. Wong holding interest per Deed of Trust recorded March 7, 1979 (Instrument No. 79-255540)
- **5 Lots Created:** 4 residential lots + Lot 5 (common area)
- **Lot 5 — HOA Common Area:** 1.06 acres gross, 0.48 acres net; partially in flood hazard zone
- **Mandatory HOA:** "Ownership of the commonly owned Lot No. 5 shall be vested in a homeowners association made up of the owners of the residential lots. Membership in the homeowners association shall be inseparable from ownership in individual lots."
- **Flood Hazard:** "PORTIONS OF LOT 5 ARE SUBJECT TO FLOOD HAZARD"
- **Street Dedications:** Palos Verdes Drive South and Sea Cove Drive (or portions thereof) remain public streets; vehicular ingress/egress from abutting lots to said streets abandoned
- **Building Restrictions in Flood/Hazard Areas:** Owners dedicated to the City of RPV the right to restrict erection of buildings or other structures within flood hazard areas; also prohibited construction of residential structures within common areas except for common recreational facilities subject to City approval
- **Soil Report:** Prepared October 22, 1976 by Western Laboratories, Jules A. Juge Jr. (R.C.E. 9697)
- **Survey Date:** November 14, 1977
- **Engineer:** Darryl E. Dalcin, R.C.E. 27797, J.H. Edwards Co., Inc.
- **City Acceptance:** City Council of RPV, by motion passed April 15, 1980, accepted the dedication of abandonment of the right of ingress and egress and also accepted the right to prohibit construction of residential structures as dedicated; also accepted building restriction rights
- **Notarized:** February 25, 1980 by Darryl E. Dalcin, Notary Public (commission expires August 9, 1983)
- **Security Deposit:** $3,000 filed with the Board of Supervisors of LA County as security for payment of taxes and special assessments
- **Basis of Bearings:** N 9 00'00" W of the centerline of Clipper Road as shown on Tract No. 14649, M.B. 345/23-26
- **City Engineer Certification:** George J. Franceschini, Deputy City Engineer, certified March 17, 1980

## Significance to WPBCA

This tract map is foundational evidence that:

1. **The HOA requirement was a condition of subdivision.** The map itself states that common area ownership vests in a homeowners association and membership is inseparable from lot ownership. This was not a voluntary arrangement — it was a recorded condition.

2. **Lot 5 (common area) was designated as flood hazard.** The City of RPV accepted the right to restrict construction in this area. Building restrictions were a condition of the subdivision.

3. **The City accepted building restriction dedications.** The City Council on April 15, 1980 formally accepted the right to prohibit residential construction in common areas and restrict building in flood hazard zones.

4. **The land's origin is a court-ordered partition** (Bixby vs. Bent), not a voluntary sale. This may affect chain-of-title arguments about what restrictions survived the 1986 reversion.
