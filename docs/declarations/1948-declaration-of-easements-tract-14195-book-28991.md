## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. Declaration No. Three — Tract 14195
This Declaration of Easements is the companion instrument to **Declaration No. Three of Protective Restrictions**, filed November 15, 1948 as Document No. 1287. Tract 14195 is mapped in Book 322, Pages 7–9.

### 2. Tract 14195 — Map Book 322, Pages 7–9
Tract 14195 is recorded in Map Book 322, Pages 7 to 9. This is the same Map Book (322) as Tract 14500 (Pages 38–41), confirming both tracts were subdivided and recorded around the same period.

### 3. Kelvin C. Vanderlip — President of Both Entities
**Kelvin C. Vanderlip** signed as President of both Palos Verdes Corporation AND Portuguese Bend Community Association, identical to the Tract 14500 declaration. This confirms the Vanderlip family controlled both the developer entity and the community governance entity simultaneously across all Portuguese Bend tracts.

### 4. Portuguese Bend Community Association (PBCA)
The PBCA approved this declaration, confirming PBCA is the governance body for both Tract 14195 and Tract 14500. This is the same community association — NOT a separate entity per tract.

### 5. PV Corp Seal — Delaware, 1925
The corporate seal reads "PALOS VERDES CORPORATION, DELAWARE, 1925" — consistent with the Tract 14500 declaration.

### 6. Block Structure — Two Blocks
Tract 14195 is organized into two blocks:
- **Block 1** — Lots 1 through 15
- **Block 2** — Lots 16 through 22

Total: 22 lots across 2 blocks.

### 7. Street Name — Monterey Drive
The drainage easement in paragraph (2) references **Monterey Drive** as a private road designated on the tract map. This is a street in the current Portuguese Bend area of RPV.

### 8. Existing Easement Holders (1948)
- **Associated Telephone Company** — Book 1829[?], Page 185
- **Southern California Edison Company** — Book 22340, Page 329
- **Palos Verdes Water Company** — Book 9511, Page 8

The Edison Company easement (Book 22340, Page 329) is the same book/page referenced in the Tract 14500 declaration, confirming a shared utility easement across both tracts. The PV Water Company easement (Book 9511, Page 8) is the same 1929 water easement already in the Cove archive.

### 9. Scan Quality — Metes and Bounds Partially Illegible
Pages 6–9 contain extensive metes and bounds descriptions for drainage, sewer, and public utility easements. Many bearing angles and distances on these pages are partially illegible due to scan quality (dark microfilm exposure, edge distortion). Entries marked with [?] require verification against the original recorded document or a higher-quality scan.

### 10. Parallel Structure to Tract 14500 Declaration
This document follows the identical structure as the Tract 14500 Declaration of Easements:
- Article X provisions quoted verbatim (Sections 1, 2, 3)
- FIRST: Road and General Easements
- SECOND: Drainage Easements
- THIRD: Sewer Easements
- FOURTH: Public Utility Easements
- FIFTH: Easements of Record
- SIXTH: Right of Release
- Signature and Approval pages

The Tract 14500 declaration uses "SEVENTH" for Right of Release; this one uses "SIXTH" because Tract 14500 has a separate "FIFTH: Bridle Trail and Public Utility Easements" section not present here.
