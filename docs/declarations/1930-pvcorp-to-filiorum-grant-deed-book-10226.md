## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. Declarations 100 and 101 — The Original CC&Rs
This deed explicitly subjects ~208 acres of Lot H to:
- **Declaration No. 100** (Book 9436, Page 155) — "Basic Protective Restrictions"
- **Declaration No. 101** (Book 9482, Page 37) — "Local Protective Restrictions"

Both were recorded in October 1929, approximately one week before the PV Corp →
PV Water Company easement. These are the **earliest known CC&R instruments** for
the Lot H territory — predating Declaration No. One (1949) by 20 years.

### 2. Filiorum Corporation — "Of the Children"
"Filiorum" is Latin for "of the children" (genitive plural of "filius"). This
strongly suggests Filiorum Corporation was a **family trust vehicle** for the
Vanderlip family. Frank A. Vanderlip (1864–1937) was the financier behind the
entire Palos Verdes development. Conveying ~208 acres to "Filiorum Corporation"
at a nominal $10 consideration in 1930 — a year after the stock market crash —
looks like an **estate planning transfer** to protect family assets.

### 3. Community Association of Palos Verdes
The deed references "Community Association of Palos Verdes, a California
corporation" as part of the governance structure. This is a **predecessor entity**
to modern HOAs in the area, including WPBCA (incorporated 1949).

### 4. Art Jury
The Art Jury is the architectural review body established by the Declarations.
This is the predecessor to modern Architectural Review Committees (ARCs).

### 5. Reversionary Interest
PV Corp retained a **reversionary interest** — breach of any restriction causes
the land to revert to PV Corp or its successors. This is an extraordinarily
strong enforcement mechanism, stronger than typical covenant enforcement.

### 6. Servitude Language
The restrictions are imposed "as a servitude in favor of said property and
each and every parcel of land therein as the dominant tenement." This is
**equitable servitude** language that runs with the land — not merely contractual.

### 7. Same Officers, Same Notary, Same Recorder
- Jay Lawyer (VP) and Donald K. Lawyer (Asst Sec) — same as 1929 water easement
- Nellie Grace Frantz — same notary, commission expires May 8, 1932
- C. L. Logan, County Recorder — same recorder
- Security Title Insurance & Guarantee Co. — same recording requestor
