## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. This Is the Missing Declaration No. 101
One of the two documents referenced in the 1930 PV Corp → Filiorum grant deed
(Book 10226, Page 170). Together with Declaration No. 100 (Book 9436, Page 155),
these are the **original CC&Rs for all of Lot H** — predating Declaration No. One
(1949) by 20 years.

### 2. Seven Parcels Cover the Entire Portuguese Bend Development Area
Declaration No. 101 doesn't restrict individual lots — it restricts seven
**massive parcels** that PV Corp planned to subdivide. The maximum building sites
table (Section 1) shows PV Corp had a density plan from the start. Every
future tract (14118, 14195, 14649, 23434, etc.) was carved from these 7 parcels.

### 3. Parcels 1-3 Were Later Conveyed to Filiorum (1930)
The metes and bounds for Parcels 1 and 2 are nearly identical to the Filiorum
grant deed. Parcel 3 in Declaration 101 (16.25 ac) is slightly larger than
Filiorum's "Parcel 3A" (11.76 ac) — Filiorum may have received a subset.
Parcels 4-7 were NOT conveyed to Filiorum and remained with PV Corp.

### 4. Restrictions Run with the Land
The servitude language is explicit: "imposed upon said realty as a servitude
in favor of said property and each and every parcel of land therein as the
dominant tenement or tenements." These restrictions bind all successors.

### 5. Art Jury = Original Architectural Review
The Art Jury (established by Declaration No. 100) has approval authority over
roof materials, building setback modifications, and architectural features.
This is the direct ancestor of modern ARCs including WPBCA's.

### 6. Class A Residence + $20,000 Minimum + 2.5 Story Limit
All 7 parcels are restricted to Class A Residential use with burned clay tile
roofs, $20,000 minimum building cost (1929 dollars ≈ $350,000 today), 25-foot
setbacks, and 2.5-story height limits.

### 7. PV Corp Reserved Modification Power
Section 6 reserves the right to modify setback and building lines due to
"irregular topography" — an acknowledgment that the Portuguese Bend landslide
terrain required flexibility. This power passed to PV Corp's successors.
