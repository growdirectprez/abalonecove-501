## Key Points

1. **Signed by Frank A. Vanderlip, Jr. as President** — this is the SON, not the father (Kelvin C. Vanderlip signed the 1949-1950 documents as President). The Lot "H" Declaration was signed by Kelvin C. Vanderlip. By 1952, Frank A. Vanderlip Jr. had taken over as President of PV Corp.

2. **John H. Robertson still serving as Assistant Secretary** — continuity of corporate officers.

3. **Explicitly incorporates Declaration No. One, the Modification, AND Declaration No. One-A** — all three are made part of the deed "as fully as though set forth at length."

4. **Explicitly carves out the racial restriction** — Subsection (m) of Section 1 of Article III is NOT incorporated into the deed. Even in 1952 (before the Civil Rights Act), PV Corp was distancing itself from the racial covenant, likely because of Shelley v. Kraemer (1948).

5. **This is the TEMPLATE deed** — this is how PV Corp conveyed every lot in Tract 14649. Each buyer received the same language. The restrictions are baked into every chain of title.

6. **"One building site"** — the deed explicitly designates Lot 1 as a single building site. This is significant for the CC&R enforcement argument: each lot is restricted to one dwelling.

7. **Declaration of Easements (Book 30051, Page 385) explicitly reserved** — the easements survive conveyance.
