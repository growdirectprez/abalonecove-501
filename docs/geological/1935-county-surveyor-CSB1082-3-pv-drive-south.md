## TRANSCRIPTION NOTES

This is a panoramic-format survey map (extremely wide aspect ratio) depicting the continuation of the planned relocation/alignment of Palos Verdes Drive South through the Portuguese Bend area. The sheet is divided into three sub-panels labeled A1, A2, and A3 of A3. The road alignment runs roughly west-to-east (left-to-right), curving through multiple tracts. The scan is somewhat higher quality than Sheet 2, but much of the fine survey data (bearings, distances, curve parameters, station numbers) remains difficult to read with full confidence. ## Road Alignment -- Palos Verdes Drive South

### General Description

The road alignment curves continuously from left (west) to right (east) across all three sub-sheets. The alignment consists of a series of tangent sections connected by horizontal curves. The road follows a sinuous path through hilly terrain, with the general trajectory bending from roughly southwest-northeast on the left side to more east-west on the right side.

### Centerline Annotation (Sub-sheet A2, Center)

A prominent annotation along the centerline in the center of the map reads:

> THIS ALIGNMENT [of] PALOS VERDES
> DRIVE SOUTH ESTABLISHED
> BY COUNTY ENGINEER 1973

This indicates the alignment shown was formally established (or re-established/confirmed) by the County Engineer in 1973, though the original survey work dates to 1935.

### Right-of-Way

Parallel lines on both sides of the centerline define the road right-of-way. The width appears to be approximately 60 feet [?] based on the scale annotations, though the exact dimension is difficult to confirm.
