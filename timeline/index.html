Timeline

113 years of land use history, 1913–2026

1882
Bixby Partition
Superior Court awards Rancho de los Palos Verdes to Jotham Bixby
1913
Vanderlip Purchase
Frank Vanderlip Sr. acquires the entire peninsula from Bixby syndicate
1923
Olmsted Master Plan
Olmsted Brothers hired to plan the peninsula — the dream
1925
PV Corporation
Vanderlip syndicate reorganizes as Palos Verdes Corporation (Delaware)
1929
Declaration 100
Art Jury and red tile district established for Portuguese Bend — same system as PVE
1929
Declaration 101
Seven parcels (~1,131 acres) placed under architectural controls
1929
Shore Club
Abalone Shore Club incorporated
1930
Filiorum Grant
PV Corp conveys coastal parcels to Filiorum Corporation
1937
Vanderlip Sr. Dies
Frank Vanderlip Sr. passes; sons not yet of age
1949
Declaration No. One
Tract 14649 established — WPBCA created, 81 lots, single-family only
1949
Ocean Access Easement
Bridle trail and walkway from Lot 1 to Pacific Ocean preserved
1950
Lot H Declaration
The negative space — all undeveloped PV Corp land except named tracts
1952
Grant Deeds
PV Corp sells individual lots, all declarations incorporated by reference
1953
Great Lakes Carbon
PV Corp stock sold for ~$9M; Vanderlip family retains 500 acres
1954
PV Corp Dissolved
Delaware dissolution — permanent, cannot be revived
1956
The Landslide
Ancient Portuguese Bend Landslide reactivates — 900 acres
1972
Condos vs. Park
Shore Club votes for shoreline park over 138–170 condominiums
1973
RPV Incorporates
City of Rancho Palos Verdes forms — to stop condo developers
1974
Abalone Cove Slide
Second major landslide begins in Abalone Cove area
1976
Coastal Act
California Coastal Act creates permanent Coastal Commission
1978
Coastal Specific Plan
Resolution 78-61 adopted — Subregion 4 policies for Abalone Cove
1980
Wong Subdivision
Tract 32977 — proper density established for 0 Clipper parcel
1986
Reversion
Tract 43725 — parcel reverts to acreage
2009
Restated Declaration
WPBCA consolidates founding documents, removes voided racial covenant
2021
Clipper Development
Ali Vahdani (Vernon, CA) acquires parcel for $2.2M
2024
Rezoning
City rezones RS-4 → RM-22 — three ordinances in 63 days
2024
FPPC Complaint
Filed against Mayor Cruikshank
2024
Petition Filed
Writ of Mandate, Case No. 24TRCP00352
2024
Emergency Declaration
Governor declares emergency for Portuguese Bend area
2025
HCD Confirms
Site 16 can be removed — city remains RHNA-compliant
2025
City Keeps Site 16
City Council votes to retain the site anyway
2026
Ground Movement
2.14 inches per week — active landslide continues
