About This Project

The Foundation

The Abalone Cove Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to preserving the documentary record of California's coastal communities. We believe public records should be publicly accessible, and that the history of how our communities were planned, built, and governed belongs to everyone.

Foundation formation in progress.

About This Research

This project began with a simple question: could technology help a small homeowners association comply with California's Davis-Stirling Act? That question led to a larger investigation — one that uncovered 113 years of recorded instruments, forgotten declarations, and a pattern of development attempts on land that has defeated every builder who tried.

The research was conducted using AI-assisted document analysis, GIS mapping, and public records from the Los Angeles County Recorder, the National Park Service, the California Secretary of State, and the City of Rancho Palos Verdes. Every claim in the article is linked to a primary source in the Evidence Room.
